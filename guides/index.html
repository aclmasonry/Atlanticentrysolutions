How openings actually perform.

Short, practical explanations of the parts of a Division 8 opening that decide whether it works — written for the questions we get asked most often on real projects.

These explain the mechanism — what a component does and why it matters — rather than reproducing performance figures. Certified data belongs with the manufacturer who tests and maintains it, so every guide ends by linking to the factory's current documentation. That way the reasoning here stays true and the numbers you use stay live.

  • For specifiers, estimators and project managers

    Sealing a fire-rated opening

    “The door is rated — so why does the perimeter matter?”

    A fire door is certified as an assembly, not as a leaf. What happens at the gap around it decides whether the assembly behaves the way it was tested to, and it is the part most often changed in the field.

  • For specifiers and project managers

    What actually drives acoustic performance

    “We specified an acoustic door. Why is the room still noisy?”

    Sound follows air. A door leaf chosen for its acoustic construction will still underperform if the perimeter leaks, and the gap under the door is usually the largest leak in the assembly.

    Solid core wood or hollow metal?

    “Which one belongs in this opening?”
